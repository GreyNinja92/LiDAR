LiDAR Enhanced AR

Augmented Reality is far from its nascent stages as a piece of technology. Today, we have several AR headsets, glasses & even smartphones with SDKs supporting AR functionality. AR requires quick computations of CNNs on device in real time to identify the depth of the scene to overlay objects, interfaces, etc.

Most of the time, the depth information gathered after running the scene through a trained CNN is still slightly inaccurate. An Evidence of this can be seen in some AR games, where stationary objects in the game move around the environment as we move the cameras around. Due to poor depth estimation, the object is not able stay attached to the ground.

Some people propose solving this issue by using a combination of Computer Vision principles and CNNs. They essentially track some features in the image and attach the object to those features. So, as the camera moves, the features will move and the object will move with the features creating an illusion that the object is stationary in virtual world. This will involve even more computation.

To fix this, we can directly capture Ground Truth Depth Information from the Environment using LiDAR. We can then use this depth information to augment the surrounding space. It'll be faster since there are much fewer CNN computations required and much more accurate. The pro model of the iPhone ships with a LiDAR scanner for quick, easy & precise AR overlays onto the camera output. LiDAR is an acronym for Light Detection and Ranging. LiDAR sensors emits light beams and measures the time it takes for the beams to reflect and come back into the sensor. It then uses this measurement to create a depth map. We've used LiDAR sensors already in several places for e.g. the Mars Rover. This helped the robot easily identify what's in front of it and make decisions about it's route accordingly.
